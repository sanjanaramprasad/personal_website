I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at Northeastern University, where I am fortunate to be advised by Byron C. Wallace.

My research focuses on rethinking factuality assessment in large language models. I characterize and evaluate confabulations (hallucinations) to understand when and why models produce unreliable outputs, with particular attention to high-stakes applications in medical, legal, and financial domains.

I have spent multiple summers as a Research Intern at Abridge AI, working with Zachary Lipton and Elisa Ferracane on factuality in clinical dialogue summarization.

Research Interests

01

Characterizing Confabulations

Understanding how hallucination patterns vary across domains and identifying novel error types like circumstantial inferences

02

Evaluating Factuality Metrics

Assessing the reliability and limitations of automatic detection methods

03

Severity-Aware Frameworks

Developing evaluation paradigms that distinguish between benign and harmful errors for risk mitigation

Prior to my Ph.D., I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer at Mya Systems, where I led conversational AI initiatives and mentored junior engineers.
